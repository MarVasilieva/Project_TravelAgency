Reminder and advices for tourists when visiting Greece


Before journey:

  • Depending on your citizenship, you may be required to have a visa; you can get an infromation it the nearest Greek consulate

  • Call your card issued bank and let them know that you are going to use the card in Greece

  • In order to access the healthcare system, tourists from European Union (EU) countries must be in possession of a European Health Card or other applicable EU document issued by the relevant social security agency. Tourists from other countries are advised to check with their insurance agency for relevant information before travelling

  • Contact your phone operator and ask if you can use your mobile phone in Greece and how it will be charged

  • Make sure you have the necessary amount of cash in case your card is denied in service by local banks



On site:

  • In order to win over the Greeks, learn a few words in Greek, they will appreciate it

  • ТThere is no need to rush in Greece. Haste will be seen here as bad manners and lack of respect for the local culture. Take an example from the Greeks, who are not in a hurry: they calmly drink coffee in the morning, have a long lunch in the afternoon, spend evenings and holidays at a richly laid table

  • From about 2 pm to 5:30 pm in the summer, Greece goes into a stupor. This is the siesta time, at which all restaurants, cafes and most shops are closed (but in tourist places they may be open, you need to check on the spot)

  • Be sure to visit the traditional tavern. In addition to the Greek salad, which you won’t pass by anyway, order moussaka in the tavern - a dish of eggplant and minced meat, and mezedes - a set of different snacks. Try the local fast food: souvlaki - shish kebab on wooden sticks, and also gyros - something like shawarma with french fries. Greeks love seafood and fish, which are deliciously grilled. For dessert, take Greek yogurt with honey and nuts and don't forget the local wines

  • Do not use private taxis, but, getting into the car of an official carrier, negotiate the fare before boarding and make sure that the driver turns on the meters

  • Documents and money are best kept in a hotel safe or placed in the most inaccessible place

  • All beaches in Greece belong to the state, so only local authorities can charge you for visiting a specially equipped beach (the price includes sunbeds and umbrellas)

  • When looking for something in Greece by address, you should keep in mind that streets with the same name can be found in different areas. Therefore, when writing down the address, specify the area and landmarks. Sometimes even taxi drivers do not know exactly where this or that street and house are located

  • During excursions, you should not try to take with you a piece of marble or a pebble from some historical building, even if it is just lying on the road. There are bound to be problems along the way.

  • Photo and filming on the territory of historical monuments and in museums is allowed only with an additional ticket

  • When visiting monasteries and churches, it is necessary to have clothes with closed shoulders, and in general to be dressed modestly: in shorts and miniskirts, they will not be allowed into the temple. True, in some monasteries guests are given special skirts and bloomers for the duration of their visit. Women don't have to cover their heads



What to bring from Greece:

  • Olive oil

  • Ouzo (aniseed vodka)

  • Olive soap. Greek olive soap has a unique scent and no chemical additives

  • Cosmetics, sea urchin washcloths

  • Chees Feta. Considered in Greece an object of special culinary pride

  • Seasonings and dressings for Greek salad



Before departure:

  • Check that you have all the necessary documents

  • In advance, check with the representative of the travel company and at the reception of the hotel you have chosen the time of delivery to the hotel of the transport that will take you to the airport

  • Check-out time in hotels is 12:00. Therefore, on the day of departure, you should fold and pack all your belongings by the specified time, pay for additional services provided to you during your stay at the hotel (telephone calls, payment of restaurant bills, etc.) and be at the specified time in the hotel lobby

  • If there is no organized transport to the airport, make sure to arrive at the airport of departure in advance, it is advisable to arrive no later than 2 hours before the flight departure to go through all pre-flight formalities (and to visit duty free)
