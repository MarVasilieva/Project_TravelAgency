Visit these wonderful islands and their destinations!

Corfu

Once an ultra-rugged destination, Corfu has become more mainstream in recent years but has also somehow managed to retain much of its rustic charm. The locals are incredibly warm and welcoming and it’s particularly good as a family holiday destination, with lots of outdoor activities including hiking, cycling, swimming and snorkelling on offer.

Mykonos

There’s so much more to Mykonos than simply late night bars and DJs. It also offers pristine beaches, fascinating history and plenty of cultural activities to keep you entertained. This popular island is a great option for solo travellers, and is also the arrival point for the ‘Sailing Greece’ yacht experience run by G Adventures, before finishing up in Mykonos.

Delos

This tiny and still largely untouched little island in the Cyclades archipelago punches well above its weight. After all, it’s supposedly the birthplace of Apollo and it offers a very special insight into the layers of Aegean history that make this part of the world so rich with culture and inspiration.

Crete

The largest island in Greece is also one of the most beautiful, with endless sandy beaches and stretches of azure coastline to explore and enjoy. Our tip: visit Crete as part of the 22-day ‘Greece in Depth’ tour offered by Bunnik Tours, which gives you a chance to spend a couple of days each in the towns of Chania and Heraklion, the two main destinations on the island.

Rhodes

There’s so much history to absorb on the island of Rhodes — and it’s certainly all worth seeing once in your life. Visit with Celestyal Cruises, who offer some fantastic add-on excursions including a medieval tour of the Acropolis of Lindos and the Citadel of the Knights, a historic walking tour in the medieval citadel of Rhodes and a transfer to the picture-perfect Tsambika Beach.

Santorini

The whitewashed volcanic island of Santorini is rocky, full of steep cliffs — and absolutely stunning. There are incredible views at every turn and one of the best ways to experience it is to do the 10km hike between the two main cities, Fira and Oia (ideally early in the morning).
